a new way to get everyone there

Drover is a mobile app for the organizers. For the people who plan their best friend's bachelorette party and the family reunion. The ones who volunteer with their kids' activities. We know how tough it is to get everyone together, but we're here to make it easier for you.

what we're doing

  • Helping You Share Schedules

    A full schedule of events can be saved to your group. You can update it whenever you need to and instantly alert everyone in your group.

  • Making Sure Everyone can Find their Way

    Need to change your meeting location? No problem! Send the location directly to your group. You can also customize the directions people get to different events.

  • Letting You Communicate with Your Group

    Not only can you send push notifications to the whole group, group members can send messages directly to the group organizer in case they have questions or need help.

And What We're Not

  • Sharing Your Contact Info

    With Drover, group organizers & members never have to share their personal contact info with each other. No phone numbers or email addresses need to be exchanged, and no social media platforms are used.

  • Overwhelming Users with Notifications

    Unlike group chats, notifications are only sent when the group leader has important information to share. No more wading through conversations to find important details.

  • Lumping Everyone Together

    Setting up group admins means the main organizer doesn't need to spend time answer all the questions that come in. Subgroups let you divide up your group and make sure everyone has the info that's appropriate for them.

How It All Started

Hi, I'm Kathleen, the founder of Drover. I've traveled the world, worked in art museums, and eventually learned coding. Additionally I've been involved with many different organizations and I've helped organize events ranging from one-day camps for girls to taking 120 adults to Munich's oktoberfest.

At age 16 I traveled around Europe with my Girl Scout troop and experienced first-hand what it's like taking a group on public transit. (That's me on the left riding the London Underground on that trip.) Years later I was living in Washington, D.C. and heard a teacher yelling at a group of kids on the Metro platform to get off in two stops. I started wondering why it wasn't possible to easily share information like that with a group and what a tool like that might be like. Those musings, along with my own experiences, formed the basis of Drover.
